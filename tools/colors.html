About Color Harmonies

Color harmony theory helps create visually pleasing combinations:

  • Complementary — opposite on the color wheel, high contrast, vibrant
  • Analogous — adjacent colors, harmonious, low contrast
  • Triadic — evenly spaced (120°), balanced, colorful
  • Split Complementary — complement + neighbors, versatile, less tension
  • Monochromatic — same hue, different saturation/lightness, elegant
  • Tetradic — two complementary pairs, rich, needs balance
